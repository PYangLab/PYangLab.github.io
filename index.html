How do cells establish identities and make cell-fate decisions?

The computational trans-regulatory biology group head by Dr. Pengyi Yang at Charles Perkins Centre (CPC), University of Sydney, focuses on understanding the trans-regulatory networks that cut across signalling, transcriptional, translational and (epi)genomic regulations in controlling cell identities and driving cell-fate decisions.

Molecular trans-regulatory networks (TRNs) comprised of cell signalling, transcriptional, translational, and (epi)genomic regulations are central to health and disease. Computational approaches are instrumental in characterising TRNs of cells at the systems level. Our research lies at the interface of bioinformatics and systems biology. We develop computational and statistical models to reconstruct signalling cascades, epigenomics, transcriptional, and proteome networks, and characterise their cross-talk and trans-regulations in various cellular processes and systems. By integrating heterogeneous -omics data with the goal of generating testable hypotheses and predictions, our research contributes to the comprehensive understanding of TRNs that underlie cellular homeostasis, proliferation, differentiation, and cell-fate decisions.

Our group are experienced in developing machine learning algorithms and statistical models for analysing the following types of data:

  • Mass spectrometry (MS)-based:
    • Proteomics
    • Phosphoproteomics
    • Lipidomics
    • Metabolomics
    • Redox proteomics
  • Next-generation sequencing (NGS)-based:
    • Bulk RNA-seq and microarray
    • Single-cell RNA-seq
    • ChIP-seq and RIP-seq of DNA/RNA binding proteins
    • RNA Polymerase II, Histones and DNaseI
    • Hi-C and ChIA-PET
  • and their cross-talk for comprehensive understanding of trans-regulatory networks.
