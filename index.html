The computational trans-omics group at Charles Perkins Centre (CPC), University of Sydney focuses on the computational study of cell signaling, epigenomic, and transcriptional regulations as a whole. Specifically, we are interested in developing computational methods and statistical models to reconstruct and characterise signaling cascades, and epigenomic and transcriptional networks that underlie cellular homeostasis, proliferation, differentiation, and cell-fate decisions.

Systematic study of complex interactions in biological networks at a global viewpoint allows us to discover fundamental principles that are not intuitive and to uncover global properties that can only be discovered by integrating interactions between individual components. We use systems biology approaches to integrate and analyze heterogeneous high-throughput “–omics” data with the goal of generating testable hypotheses and predictions. Our objective is to discover critical cell signaling pathways that regulate epigenomic landscapes and gene expression programs controlling cell type identity. Results from these studies will contribute to the comprehensive understanding of the cross-talk among cell signaling, epigenomic, and transcriptional regulations.

Our research focuses include:

  • Analysis of mass spectrometry (MS)-based:
    • Proteomics data
    • Phosphoproteomics data
  • Analysis of Transcriptomics and Epigenomics data including:
    • Bulk and single-cell RNA-seq
    • ChIP-seq and DNaseI
    • Hi-C and ChIA-PET
  • And their cross-talk for comprehensive understanding of trans-omics networks.
